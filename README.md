# Multi-Target Random Picker

[English](README.md) • [Italiano](README_it.md) • [Español](README_es.md) • [Français](README_fr.md) • [Deutsch](README_de.md) • [中文](README_zh.md) • [日本語](README_ja.md)

The ultimate web application to handle random drawings at school quickly, transparently, and modernly. Forget old paper slips or slow generators: this tool is designed to deliver a smooth, instant, and fully responsive interface for any device.

Whether it is for a last-minute oral exam, splitting the class into working groups, or choosing presentation turns, Multi-Target Random Picker does all the heavy lifting for you, right inside your browser.

---

## What you can do

* **On-the-Fly Number Drawings**
  Choose your range (e.g., from 1 to 30), decide how many numbers to draw together, and launch. Zero waiting time, instant results.

* **Custom Lists of Names**
  Paste or type your class roster (one name per line). The app automatically counts how many students are present and ready for the drawing.

* **"Never Duplicate" Mode**
  Enable the anti-repetition algorithm: drawn names or numbers are automatically excluded from the following rounds and displayed in a dedicated section, ensuring no one is picked twice.

* **History Always Under Control**
  The application remembers the last 15 drawings made, complete with exact timestamps and modes, saving everything locally on your device even if you close the page.

## Project Strengths

<details>
<summary><b>Why is it so convenient and lightweight? (Technical details)</b></summary>

* **Everything in a single file**: The whole application lives inside `index.html`. No messy folders or scattered assets.
  
* **Zero installations**: No servers or configurations required. Just double-click the file and you are ready to go on PC, tablet, or smartphone.
  
* **Local persistence**: It leverages your browser's internal storage (`localStorage`) so you never lose your drawing history.
  
* **No external dependencies**: It works completely offline and does not require downloading heavy third-party packages.

</details>
